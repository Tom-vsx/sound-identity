# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Music listeners who want a poetic, personalised "musical identity" — not a Spotify stats page. They are thoughtful, culturally curious, the kind of person who reads liner notes and has opinions about mastering. Age 22–38. They use this alone, at night, on a phone or laptop. They share results with people who will understand the reference.

## Product Purpose

Sound Identity is a short onboarding quiz (5 steps: genres, artists, listening context, emotional intent, era) that ends with a revealed tarot-style card — a unique archetype that reflects the user's relationship to music. The card is the product. The reveal is the moment. Everything before it is ceremony.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Atmospheric. Intimate.

The voice is that of a music magazine that actually gets you — not a recommendation algorithm, not a horoscope. Think Pitchfork editorial meets quiet-luxury design. Feels like it was made by someone with taste, not by AI.

## Anti-references

- Spotify Wrapped (too data-driven, gamified, loud)
- Apple Music (clinical, corporate blue)
- Last.fm (nostalgic but dated)
- Generic "personality quiz" UIs (BuzzFeed energy)
- Neon/synthwave aesthetics (overdone in music apps)
- Heavy glassmorphism (feels like 2021 SaaS)

## Design Principles

1. **Ceremony before reveal.** Every interaction builds toward the card. Nothing is filler.
2. **Restraint as sophistication.** What's left out is the point. Small details over loud gestures.
3. **Readable first, atmospheric second.** The atmosphere never beats the legibility.
4. **Dark for a reason.** Users encounter this late at night, alone, with headphones. The dark palette is contextually correct.
5. **Every word earns its place.** Copy is editorial, not UX-speak.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All interactive text ≥ 14px. All body/label text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 on dark background. Touch targets ≥ 44×44px. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected. Focus states visible.
